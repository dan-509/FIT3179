What is 'Trade Balance'?

Trade Balance is used to measure the relative strength of a country's economy. It is calculated as the sum of the country's exports, minus its imports. This graph normalises the trade balance, by using the trade balance as a percentage country's GDP (Gross Domestic Product). In general, the higher the percentage is, the better.
